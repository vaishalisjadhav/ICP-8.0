Mahendra Singh Dhoni
                                                                                                                                 The Captain Cool!


                                                                                                                                                      

Mahendra Singh Dhoni is popularly known as Mahi among his fans. He was born on July 7, in the year 1981. His mothers name is Devaki Dev, and his fathers name is Pan Singh. His birthplace is Ranchi in Jharkhand, which was Bihar at the time. His parents earlier lived in Uttarakhand, but they moved to Jharkhand because his father worked there. MS Dhoni has one brother and one sister as well.
Dhoni was a student of DAV Jawahar Vidya Mandir School in Ranchi. Initially, he played badminton and football in his school days and excelled in both sports. He was a district-level player. Dhoni was first sent to play cricket by his football coach.
This was the first time Dhoni played cricket, but his skills were outstanding as a wicket-keeper. His journey in the cricket world started as a wicket-keeper. After completing his studies in the 10th standard, Dhoni focused more on cricket.
Dhoni also worked as a Train Ticket Examiner in West Bengal. Dhoni is known for being simple, honest, and yet mischievous.
Dhoni was selected for the Bihar U-19 team during the 1998-99 season. He won his first Ranji Trophy when he was 18 years old, during the 1999-2000 season. Dhoni played quite seriously for the national selection in 2004 and helped the East Zone team to win Deodhar Trophy and Duleep Trophy.
Later, he came to be known as one of the best players when he scored two centuries against Pakistan and Kenya in the triangular tournament. He was then recognised by the BCCI when they were searching for talents in small towns. He was also observed by the Bengal Captain, PC Poddar, in the 1960s when playing for the Jharkhand team. The report was forwarded to the National Cricket Academy right away.
Since then, MS Dhoni has been consistently giving the best performances. His achievements are an inspiration not only to the cricket players but to the entire nation.
